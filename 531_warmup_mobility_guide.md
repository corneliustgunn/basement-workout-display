# Warm-Up & Mobility Guide

**Program:** 5/3/1 BBB with Periodized Cardio  
**Purpose:** Injury prevention, joint preparation, recovery enhancement  
**Three layers:** Pre-lift dynamic warm-up · Post-lift Peloton stretch · Sunday yoga

---

## Layer 1: Pre-Lift Dynamic Warm-Up (~5 min)

Perform before your barbell ramp-up sets. No static stretching — all dynamic movement to prime joints for the specific demands of the day. Move with control, not speed.

---

### Monday — Squat Day

**Target areas:** Hips, ankles, thoracic spine

| Order | Exercise                          | Reps / Duration | Cues                                              |
|-------|-----------------------------------|-----------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | Bodyweight Squat                  | 10 reps         | Full depth. Pause briefly at the bottom.           |
| 2     | Hip 90/90 Switches                | 5 per side      | Sit on floor, both knees at 90°, rotate side to side. Keep torso tall. |
| 3     | Ankle Rocks (half-kneeling)       | 10 per side     | Front foot flat, drive knee over toes. Feel the stretch in the calf and ankle. |
| 4     | Cat-Cow                           | 8 reps          | Slow, full range. Inhale on cow (arch), exhale on cat (round). |
| 5     | Goblet Squat Hold (15 lb DB)      | 20 sec          | Sit into the bottom of a squat and hold. Use the DB as a counterbalance. Push knees out with elbows. |

**Why this sequence:** Squats demand hip flexion, ankle dorsiflexion, and thoracic extension simultaneously. The 90/90 switches open the hip capsule in both internal and external rotation. Ankle rocks address the dorsiflexion that limits squat depth. The goblet hold integrates everything under light load before you touch the barbell.

---

### Tuesday — Bench Day

**Target areas:** Shoulder capsule, rotator cuff, thoracic spine

| Order | Exercise                          | Reps / Duration | Cues                                              |
|-------|-----------------------------------|-----------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | Band Pull-Apart                   | 15 reps         | Light band, arms straight, squeeze shoulder blades at end range. |
| 2     | Arm Circles (small → large)       | 10 each direction | Start with small circles, progressively widen. Both forward and backward. |
| 3     | Wall Slides                       | 10 reps         | Back and arms against wall. Slide arms overhead, keeping contact with the wall. If you can't maintain contact, reduce range. |
| 4     | Push-Up to Downward Dog           | 8 reps          | Perform a push-up, then press hips up and back into downward dog. Feel the lat and shoulder stretch at the top. |
| 5     | Dead Hang (pull-up bar)           | 20 sec          | Relaxed grip, let the shoulders open. Breathe. |

**Why this sequence:** Bench press requires scapular stability and sufficient shoulder extension to set an arch. Band pull-aparts activate the rear delts and lower traps (critical for scapular retraction). Wall slides assess and improve overhead mobility. The dead hang decompresses the shoulder and opens the lats, which can restrict bench setup if tight.

---

### Thursday — Deadlift Day

**Target areas:** Hamstrings, hip hinge pattern, thoracic spine

| Order | Exercise                          | Reps / Duration | Cues                                              |
|-------|-----------------------------------|-----------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | Hip Hinge with Broomstick/PVC     | 10 reps         | Hold stick along spine (touching head, upper back, tailbone). Hinge at hips keeping all three contact points. This grooves the pattern. |
| 2     | World's Greatest Stretch           | 5 per side      | Lunge forward, plant both hands inside the front foot, rotate and reach to the ceiling with the inside hand. Covers hip flexors, t-spine rotation, hamstrings, and adductors in one movement. |
| 3     | Leg Swings (front-to-back)         | 10 per side     | Hold the rack for balance. Swing through full range with a straight leg. Controlled, not ballistic. |
| 4     | Cat-Cow                           | 8 reps          | Same as Monday. Mobilizes the entire spine before axial loading. |
| 5     | Bodyweight Romanian Deadlift       | 8 reps          | Slow, feeling the hamstring stretch at the bottom. Squeeze glutes at the top. Pattern rehearsal before loading. |

**Why this sequence:** Deadlifts demand hamstring length at the bottom position and hip hinge integrity throughout. The broomstick drill teaches the hinge without load. World's greatest stretch is the single best multi-joint warm-up movement that exists — it opens everything the deadlift needs. Bodyweight RDLs rehearse the specific movement pattern you'll load during BBB sets.

---

### Friday — OHP Day

**Target areas:** Shoulder flexion, lats, thoracic extension

| Order | Exercise                          | Reps / Duration | Cues                                              |
|-------|-----------------------------------|-----------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | Band Pull-Apart                   | 15 reps         | Same as Tuesday. Activates posterior shoulder before pressing. |
| 2     | Band Dislocate                    | 10 reps         | Wide grip on a light band. Arc it from hips to behind your back and return. Use a grip wide enough that there's zero pain. If your bands are too heavy, skip this and double the wall slides. |
| 3     | Wall Slides                       | 10 reps         | Same as Tuesday. Overhead pressing demands the most shoulder mobility of any barbell lift. |
| 4     | Thoracic Extension over Towel      | 10 reps         | Roll up a towel, place it on the floor. Lie on it at mid-back level, hands behind head, extend over the towel. This directly targets the t-spine extension needed to press overhead without compensating through the lower back. |
| 5     | Dead Hang (pull-up bar)           | 20 sec          | Opens lats and shoulder flexion range. Tight lats are the #1 limiter of overhead pressing mobility. |

**Why this sequence:** Seated OHP at 80–85° still requires significant shoulder flexion and thoracic extension. Tight lats pull the shoulder into internal rotation and limit overhead range — the dead hang and band work directly address this. The thoracic extension drill is especially important since you'll be pressing seated, which eliminates the ability to lean back slightly that standing OHP allows.

---

### After the Warm-Up

Proceed directly to barbell ramp-up sets:

| Set | % of TM | Reps |
|-----|---------|------|
| 1   | 40%     | 5    |
| 2   | 50%     | 5    |
| 3   | 60%     | 3    |

Then begin your 5/3/1 working sets.

## Layer 2: Post-Lift Peloton Stretch (5–10 min)

Complete after your final accessory exercise. Static stretching is appropriate here since the session is over and you're not about to produce force.

| Lifting Day          | Peloton Class Type           | Duration | Focus Areas                                  |
|----------------------|------------------------------|----------|----------------------------------------------|
| Monday (Squat)       | Lower Body Stretch           | 5–10 min | Quads, hamstrings, hip flexors, glutes       |
| Tuesday (Bench)      | Upper Body Stretch           | 5–10 min | Chest, lats, shoulders, triceps              |
| Thursday (Deadlift)  | Lower Body Stretch           | 5–10 min | Hamstrings, glutes, hip flexors, lower back  |
| Friday (OHP)         | Upper Body Stretch           | 5–10 min | Shoulders, lats, chest, thoracic spine       |

**Progression:** Start with 5 min classes. Move to 10 min when your schedule allows. Consistency matters more than duration — four 5-minute sessions per week beats one 20-minute session.

**Why this matters for the program:** This program has substantial pressing volume (BBB bench + BBB OHP + close-grip bench + overhead tricep extensions). Without dedicated chest and shoulder stretching, the anterior shoulder and pecs tighten progressively over weeks, eventually limiting overhead range and increasing impingement risk. The post-lift stretch is a pressure valve that prevents this from accumulating.

## Layer 3: Sunday Yoga (20–30 min)

Sunday is your rest day. A single yoga session turns it into active recovery without adding training stress.

### What to Search on Peloton

Search for classes tagged **"Slow Flow"** or **"Beginner Yoga Flow"** in the 20–30 min range. Avoid classes described as "Power Yoga" or "Yoga Bootcamp" — those are workouts, not recovery.

Good instructors and class styles to look for:
- Anything emphasizing **hip openers** and **shoulder stretches**
- Classes that include **pigeon pose** (deep hip flexor/glute stretch)
- Classes that include **thread the needle** (thoracic rotation)
- Classes that include **downward dog** and **child's pose** (lat and shoulder stretch)

### What Yoga Specifically Addresses in This Program

| Program Demand                         | Yoga Benefit                                         |
|----------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Heavy squats + deadlifts → tight hip flexors | Hip openers (pigeon, lizard, low lunge)            |
| BBB volume → compressed thoracic spine  | Spinal rotation and extension (twists, cat-cow, cobra) |
| Constant hamstring loading (RDL, BSS)  | Forward folds and hamstring lengthening              |
| Heavy pressing → tight chest/anterior shoulder | Chest openers and shoulder stretches              |
| 6 days/week training → elevated sympathetic tone | Breathwork and parasympathetic downregulation     |

The last point is underrated. Training 6 days a week keeps your nervous system in a heightened state. Yoga's breathwork component (specifically extended exhales) actively shifts you toward recovery. This is not woo — it's vagal nerve stimulation, and it measurably improves HRV and sleep quality.

### Minimum Viable Dose

If 20–30 min feels like too much on your rest day, a **10 min Peloton yoga class** still provides meaningful benefit. Prioritize hip and shoulder-focused classes at this shorter duration. The floor is 10 min once per week — below that, the adaptations don't accumulate session to session.

## Equipment Needed

Everything uses items you already have:

- **Resistance band** (light): Pull-aparts, dislocates, Pallof press warm-up
- **15 lb dumbbell**: Goblet squat hold warm-up
- **Pull-up bar**: Dead hangs
- **Towel**: Thoracic extension roll (or a foam roller if you have one)
- **Broomstick or PVC pipe**: Hip hinge drill (or just use an empty barbell)

---

## Signs You Need More Mobility Work

If you notice any of these, increase the relevant warm-up duration or add a second yoga session:

- **Can't hit full squat depth without heel rise** → More ankle rocks and goblet squat holds
- **Shoulders pinch at the bottom of bench press** → More dead hangs and wall slides, longer upper body stretch
- **Lower back rounds at the bottom of deadlift** → More hip hinge drills and world's greatest stretch
- **Can't press overhead without excessive back arch** → More thoracic extension work and lat stretching
- **General stiffness that doesn't clear after warm-up** → Add a second 10 min yoga session midweek (Wednesday evening works well)
